A good camp pillow can make the difference between a night of tossing and turning and waking up refreshed on the trail. While many backpackers try to get by with a stuff-sack of clothes, dedicated camp pillows offer real support, consistent loft, and a cleaner sleep surface. The challenge is balancing weight, packed size, comfort, and cost. To choose the right one, think about your sleep style and camping type. Side sleepers need more thickness (typically 4 to 5 inches), while stomach sleepers want something low-profile. Ultralight hikers will prioritize weight and packed volume, often accepting inflatable pillows that can be a bit noisy or lack side support.
